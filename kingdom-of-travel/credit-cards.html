Best Canadian Travel Credit Cards of 2025

Updated: May 2025 12 cards ranked By KingdomOfTravel Editorial

We evaluate every major Canadian travel credit card on welcome bonus value, everyday earn rates, travel perks, and annual fee payoff. This list is updated monthly whenever cards change their offers.

👑 Pro Tip

The single biggest mistake Canadians make is choosing a card based on the annual fee alone. A $139/yr card with a 50,000-point welcome bonus is worth roughly $1,000 in business class flights — far more than the fee suggests.

American Express Cards

1. Amex Cobalt — Best for Everyday Earners

The Cobalt earns 5 Membership Rewards points per dollar on food and drinks (restaurants, cafes, food delivery, and grocery stores). For most Canadians, this makes it the highest-earning card in everyday spending. Points transfer to Aeroplan at 1:1, making each point worth roughly 1.5–2 cents in business class value.

  • Welcome offer: Up to 15,000 MR points (1,250/mo for 12 months)
  • Annual fee: $12.99/month ($155.88/yr effectively)
  • Earn rate: 5x dining, 3x streaming, 2x travel, 1x everything else

2. Amex Platinum — Best Premium Card

Canada's most prestigious travel card. The $200 travel credit alone nearly offsets the $799 annual fee, and the 100,000-point welcome bonus (worth $2,000+ toward business class) makes year one extraordinary value. You also get automatic Marriott Bonvoy Gold Elite and Hilton Honors Gold status.

  • Welcome offer: Up to 100,000 MR points
  • Annual fee: $799/yr
  • Key perk: Priority Pass membership + Amex Global Lounge access (1,300+ lounges)

TD Bank Cards

3. TD Aeroplan Visa Infinite — Best Aeroplan Card

If you fly Air Canada even occasionally, this card pays for itself. First checked bag free for the cardholder means a family of four saves $60 each way — roughly $120/round trip. The 50,000-point welcome offer is enough for a one-way business class seat to Europe when you know the sweet spots.

  • Welcome offer: Up to 50,000 Aeroplan points
  • Annual fee: $139/yr (first year often waived)
  • Key perk: Preferred pricing + first bag free on Air Canada

RBC Cards

4. RBC Avion Visa Infinite — Best Flexible Points

Avion points transfer to British Airways Avios, WestJet Dollars, and several other programs, giving you redemption flexibility few cards match. The 55,000-point welcome offer is solid, and the 1.25x earn rate on everyday spending is above average for a bank card.

  • Welcome offer: Up to 55,000 Avion points
  • Annual fee: $120/yr
  • Key perk: Transfer to 11+ airline and hotel programs

How We Rank Cards

Our rankings weight welcome bonus value (40%), ongoing earn rate (35%), travel perks (15%), and annual fee fairness (10%). We only recommend cards we would personally carry. We earn a referral fee if you apply through our links — this never influences our rankings.
