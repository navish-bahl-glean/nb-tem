The Best Canadian Travel Credit Cards of 2025

Updated: May 2025 10 min read By KingdomOfTravel Editorial

2025 has brought record-high welcome bonuses, improved earn rates, and some genuinely exciting new perks. Here is our definitive ranked list — updated monthly — of every Canadian travel credit card worth carrying.

👑 How to Use This List

If you're just starting out, apply for the Amex Cobalt first — it has the best ongoing earn rate in Canada. If you already have the Cobalt, the TD Aeroplan Visa Infinite should be your next card.

Tier 1: Essential Cards (Everyone Should Have One)

#1 — Amex Cobalt

Still the undisputed best everyday earner in Canada. Five points per dollar on food and drinks is unmatched — no other card comes close at this price point. The $12.99/month fee means you're paying roughly $156/year, but most cardholders earn well over $500/year in travel value from spending alone.

  • Welcome offer: Up to 15,000 MR points (1,250 pts/mo for 12 months)
  • Earn: 5x food & drinks, 3x streaming, 2x travel & transit, 1x everything else
  • Annual fee: $12.99/month
  • Best for: Everyday spending, dining, groceries

#2 — TD Aeroplan Visa Infinite

The best card for Air Canada flyers. First checked bag free for the cardholder and one companion on the same booking — that's up to $120 saved per round trip for a couple. The 50,000-point welcome offer has been at all-time highs throughout 2025.

  • Welcome offer: Up to 50,000 Aeroplan points (worth ~$1,000 in economy, ~$2,000 in business)
  • Earn: 1.5x on Air Canada, 1.5x on grocery, 1x everything else
  • Annual fee: $139/year (often waived year one)
  • Best for: Air Canada flyers, families checking bags

Tier 2: Premium Cards (For Frequent Travellers)

#3 — Amex Platinum

Canada's best premium travel card. The $200 annual travel credit is automatic and easy to use — just book any flight and the credit applies. Priority Pass membership covers you and a guest at 1,300+ airport lounges worldwide. The 100,000-point welcome bonus (the highest you'll find anywhere) is worth approximately $2,000–$3,000 in business class value.

  • Welcome offer: Up to 100,000 MR points
  • Earn: 3x dining worldwide, 2x travel, 1x everything else
  • Annual fee: $799/year
  • Best for: High spenders, frequent travellers, lounge addicts

Tier 3: Strong Supporting Cards

#4 — RBC Avion Visa Infinite

Avion points transfer to British Airways Avios, making this card excellent for booking short-haul business class on BA partners. A YYZ–LHR business class on BA costs around 62,500 Avios — achievable with a welcome bonus plus a few months of spending.

#5 — CIBC Aeroplan Visa Infinite

Nearly identical to the TD Aeroplan card but worth holding both for the additional welcome bonus. The "status qualifying miles" feature on CIBC's card helps you earn Air Canada status faster if you fly regularly.

Cards We Don't Recommend

We've intentionally excluded several heavily marketed cards from this list. Any card with cash back or points redeemable only at a fixed rate (1–1.5 cents per point) will never deliver the 8–12 cent per point value achievable with premium flight redemptions. If a card doesn't let you transfer to Aeroplan, Marriott, or another major program, it's difficult to justify over the cards above.

How to Apply Without Hurting Your Credit Score

Applying for multiple cards in a short period can temporarily reduce your credit score by 5–10 points per hard inquiry. This rebounds within 6–12 months. Space applications 3–4 months apart if you're concerned. Always pay your balance in full — carrying a balance costs far more than any points are worth.
