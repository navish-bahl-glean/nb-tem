Points 101: The Complete Beginner's Guide to Travel Rewards

Updated: May 2025 15 min read By KingdomOfTravel Editorial

If you've ever wondered how some people manage to fly business class to Tokyo for "free" or spend a week at a Maldives resort without paying rack rates, the answer is almost always the same: they've mastered the art of collecting and redeeming travel rewards points.

This guide will teach you everything you need to get started — from understanding what points actually are, to earning your first 100,000 points, to booking a redemption that genuinely blows your mind.

What Are Travel Points and Miles?

Travel points and miles are currencies issued by airlines, hotels, and credit card companies. You earn them by spending money, and you redeem them for free (or heavily discounted) flights, hotel stays, and other travel experiences.

In Canada, the most important currencies to know are:

  • Aeroplan Miles — Air Canada's loyalty currency. Books flights on Air Canada and 40+ partner airlines.
  • American Express Membership Rewards (MR) — The most flexible points in Canada. Transfer to Aeroplan, Marriott Bonvoy, and more.
  • RBC Avion Points — Transfer to British Airways Avios, WestJet, and others.
  • Marriott Bonvoy Points — Hotel nights at 8,000+ properties. Also converts to airline miles.
  • Hilton Honors Points — Hotel nights at 7,000+ properties.
👑 Key Insight

The most valuable skill in points travel is knowing which currency to collect. Amex MR points are the most flexible because they transfer to multiple programs — meaning you don't lock yourself into one airline or hotel chain.

How Do You Earn Points?

There are four main ways to earn travel points:

1. Credit Card Welcome Bonuses (The Fastest Way)

When you open a new travel credit card, the bank offers a large one-time bonus for meeting a minimum spend requirement. These bonuses are enormous compared to ongoing earning. A typical welcome offer gives you 50,000–100,000 points — enough for a one-way business class ticket to Europe — just for spending $3,000–$5,000 on the card in the first three months.

2. Everyday Credit Card Spending

Your credit card earns points on every purchase. The best cards earn 5x points on categories like dining and groceries (Amex Cobalt), while others earn 1.5x–2x across the board. Over time, consistent spending builds a sizeable balance.

3. Flying and Staying at Hotels

Flights earn Aeroplan miles and hotel stays earn Bonvoy or Hilton points based on dollars spent. Reaching elite status (by flying or staying enough nights per year) dramatically increases your earn rate.

4. Shopping Portals and Bonus Offers

Aeroplan's eStore lets you earn bonus miles when shopping at hundreds of retailers online. Amex Offers and TD Bonus Offers provide temporary bonus earning at specific merchants.

How Do You Redeem Points for Maximum Value?

Redeeming for premium cabin flights (business and first class) gives the best value. Here's why: a Toronto–Tokyo business class ticket might cost $8,000 in cash. But it might cost 75,000 Aeroplan points — points you could have earned in a year of normal credit card spending.

That works out to roughly 10 cents per point in value — versus the 1–2 cents per point you'd get redeeming for economy tickets or merchandise.

👑 The Golden Rule

Never redeem points for merchandise, gift cards, or cash back. The value is a fraction of what you'd get from premium flights. Collect patiently, redeem lavishly.

Your First 100,000 Points: A Step-by-Step Plan

Here's a realistic path to earning 100,000 points in your first year:

  • Step 1: Apply for the Amex Cobalt. Earn up to 15,000 MR points in your first year through monthly bonuses.
  • Step 2: Apply for the TD Aeroplan Visa Infinite. Earn 50,000 Aeroplan points via the welcome offer.
  • Step 3: Put all everyday dining and groceries on the Cobalt (5x), and Air Canada purchases on the TD card (1.5x).
  • Step 4: Transfer your Cobalt MR points to Aeroplan at 1:1 when you have a specific redemption in mind.
  • Step 5: Book your first business class flight. Tokyo from Vancouver costs 55,000 Aeroplan points one-way on ANA.

Common Beginner Mistakes to Avoid

  • Carrying a balance on your credit card — interest charges will always outweigh point value
  • Redeeming too soon for low-value rewards before understanding premium cabin value
  • Spreading points across too many programs instead of focusing on 1–2 currencies
  • Ignoring welcome bonuses — they're worth 5x more than a year of spending earning
  • Hoarding points indefinitely — devaluations happen; redeem within 12–18 months of collecting
